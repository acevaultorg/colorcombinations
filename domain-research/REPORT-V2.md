## My revised pick

**`sumitome.com`** — my new #1. Here's why it beats irolexicon.com:

1. **Shorter** — 8 chars stem vs 10.
2. **More poetic sound** — "soo-mee-toh-may" has musical rhythm.
3. **"Tome" is a real English word** — signals prestige, literary weight, book-ness.
4. **Sumi-e association** — traditional Japanese ink painting is a globally recognized art form.
5. **Logo-friendly** — short, balanced, serif-able.
6. **Tagline ready** — "The Sumitome: historical color combinations, curated."
7. **Cross-cultural bridge** — Japanese word + Western word, both understood independently.

Runner-ups: `irolexicon.com` (most literal), `hanatome.com` (softest, flower-first), `wadacodex.com` (most direct Wada claim).
